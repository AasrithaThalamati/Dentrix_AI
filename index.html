Understanding the
Root Canal

Anatomy Legend

① Pulp Chamber

The coronal portion of the root canal system containing nerve tissue, blood vessels, and pulpal stroma. AI evaluates the orifice width as a reference for coronal third density.

② Gutta-Percha Fill

The radiopaque obturating material visible as a dense white column on IOPA. ObturaScore AI analyzes homogeneity, void presence, and taper continuity throughout this column.

③ Apical Seal

The terminus of the obturation relative to the radiographic apex. Optimal termination: 0–2mm short. Short fill >2mm scores penalty. Overextension receives lowest length score.

④ PDL Space

The periodontal ligament space appears as a thin radiolucent line around the root. Widening may indicate periapical pathology — a context flag in the clinical interpretation output.

⑤ Taper Continuity

The gradual narrowing from the orifice to apex. AI uses geometric edge detection to measure taper angle deviation. Abrupt changes, ledges, or transportation are flagged as irregularities.
