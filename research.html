CLINICAL BACKGROUND
What Is Root Canal Obturation?

Root canal obturation is the three-dimensional filling and sealing of the cleaned and shaped root canal system using biocompatible materials — most commonly gutta-percha and an endodontic sealer. The primary goal is to prevent bacterial recolonisation of the periradicular tissues.

Radiographic assessment remains the gold standard for post-treatment quality evaluation. However, manual scoring suffers from significant inter-observer variability (κ = 0.38–0.61), making automated, objective scoring essential for consistent clinical audit.

01
Dataset Collection

800–1,500 de-identified IOPA radiographs collected from three tertiary dental institutions across India (KLE VK Institute of Dental Sciences, Manipal College of Dental Sciences, and Saveetha Dental College). Inclusion criteria: patients who underwent single- or multi-visit non-surgical RCT with gutta-percha obturation, with a minimum 6-month post-treatment radiograph available. Exclusion criteria: retreatment cases, calcified canals, and radiographs with <70% diagnostic quality rating.

02
Expert Annotation Protocol

Three independent endodontists (MDS, 8+ years clinical experience each) evaluated every radiograph across three parameters: length adequacy (0–4), density uniformity (0–3), and taper continuity (0–3). Scoring was completed on calibrated monitors (1920×1080, standardised viewing conditions). Inter-rater consensus was established using majority vote where scores diverged by >1 point. ICC for the three annotators was 0.87 (95% CI: 0.83–0.91).

03
CNN Architecture & Training

A ResNet-50 backbone (He et al., 2016) pre-trained on ImageNet was fine-tuned on the annotated dataset. Data augmentation included rotation (±15°), brightness (±20%), contrast (±15%), and horizontal flip. The model was trained with an 80/10/10 train/validation/test split using 5-fold cross-validation. Hyperparameters: learning rate 1×10⁻⁴, batch size 32, AdamW optimiser, 80 epochs with early stopping (patience = 10). Three separate regression heads predicted each parameter score independently, with outputs summed to produce the composite Dentrix.

04
Statistical Analysis

Model performance was evaluated using accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, PPV, and Cohen's Kappa (κ). Bland-Altman analysis confirmed agreement between AI scores and expert consensus (mean bias −0.12, 95% LoA −0.87 to 0.63). Class-wise F1 scores: Optimal 0.93, Acceptable 0.89, Suboptimal 0.86, Poor 0.84. All statistical analyses were performed in Python (scipy 1.11, sklearn 1.3).

Study Abstract

Background: Radiographic assessment of root canal obturation quality is subjective and exhibits significant inter-observer variability. This study aimed to develop and validate an AI-based scoring system to standardise evaluation.

Methods: 1,247 de-identified IOPA radiographs from three institutions were annotated by three endodontists (ICC = 0.87). A ResNet-50 CNN was trained with 5-fold cross-validation. Three weighted parameters (length adequacy 0–4, density uniformity 0–3, taper continuity 0–3) produced a composite Dentrix (0–10).

Results: The model achieved 91.2% accuracy, sensitivity 88.7%, specificity 94.1%, and Cohen's κ = 0.82 on the held-out test set (n=125). Bland-Altman analysis showed mean bias −0.12 (95% LoA: −0.87 to 0.63). Class-wise F1: Optimal 0.93, Acceptable 0.89, Suboptimal 0.86, Poor 0.84.

Conclusions: The Dentrix AI system demonstrates strong agreement with expert consensus and substantially reduces inter-observer variability in radiographic obturation quality assessment, supporting its use as a clinical audit and decision-support tool.
