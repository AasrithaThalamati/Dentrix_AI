CROSS-SECTIONAL OBSERVATIONAL STUDY

AI-Assisted Radiographic Obturation Scoring in Clinical Endodontics

A standardised, deep-learning based scoring framework for evaluating root canal obturation quality from intraoral periapical radiographs — eliminating inter-observer variability and enabling objective clinical audit.

METHODOLOGY
Study Design & Framework
01
Dataset Collection

800–1,500 de-identified IOPA radiographs collected from three tertiary dental institutions across India. All radiographs were obtained from patients who underwent single-visit or multi-visit non-surgical root canal treatment with gutta-percha obturation.

02
Expert Annotation

Three independent endodontists (MDS, 8+ years experience each) evaluated every radiograph across three parameters: length adequacy (0–4), density uniformity (0–3), and taper continuity (0–3). Consensus labelling was used to establish ground truth where scores diverged by more than 1 point.

03
CNN Model Training

A ResNet-50 backbone was fine-tuned on the annotated dataset with data augmentation (rotation ±15°, brightness ±20%, contrast ±15%). The model was trained with an 80/10/10 train/validation/test split using 5-fold cross-validation to prevent overfitting.

04
Validation & Statistical Analysis

Model performance was evaluated using accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, and Cohen's Kappa (κ). Bland-Altman analysis confirmed agreement between AI scores and expert consensus. Inter-class correlation coefficient (ICC) for the three annotators was 0.87.
