Understanding the
Root Canal

Hover over each labelled structure to understand what Dentrix AI evaluates. Click a label to learn more.

Anatomy Legend

① Pulp Chamber

The coronal portion of the root canal system containing nerve tissue, blood vessels, and pulpal stroma. AI evaluates the orifice width as a reference for coronal third density.

② Gutta-Percha Fill

The radiopaque obturating material visible as a dense white column on IOPA. Dentrix AI analyzes homogeneity, void presence, and taper continuity throughout this column.

③ Apical Seal

The terminus of the obturation relative to the radiographic apex. Optimal termination: 0–2mm short. Short fill >2mm scores penalty. Overextension receives lowest length score.

④ PDL Space

The periodontal ligament space appears as a thin radiolucent line around the root. Widening may indicate periapical pathology — a context flag in the clinical interpretation output.

⑤ Taper Continuity

The gradual narrowing from the orifice to apex. AI uses geometric edge detection to measure taper angle deviation. Abrupt changes, ledges, or transportation are flagged as irregularities.

HOW THE AI WORKS

Scoring Methodology

The CNN analyses radiographic pixel density maps using edge-detection convolutions tuned for hydroxyapatite vs. gutta-percha attenuation coefficients.

🧠
Training dataset: The model was trained on 1,500+ annotated IOPA radiographs, each labelled by consensus of 3 independent endodontists. Inter-examiner agreement of κ ≥ 0.82 was required before inclusion.
